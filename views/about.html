About the research

Questions were then formed around ‘trust’ online and how a user is likely to discern a trustworthy site, when so many legitimate websites employ techniques such as 'dark patterns'[1] to elicit their desired responses from users.

The research aims to investigate the different ways in which users interact with a web based form, based on a number of different factors surrounding its design.

[1]http://wiki.darkpatterns.org/Home/index.html?title=index.html

Hypothesis

A significant number of users will be convinced of the site’s ‘trustworthiness’ based purely on design and aesthetics.

Inspiration

Following the initial spark of an idea for this project, Lalita spoke with a number of industry and academic peers who inspired this work through their recommendations and knowledge of the subject.

These recommendations included;

  • Dan Ariely - Predictably Irrational.
  • Harry Brignull - Dark Patterns.
  • Chris Brogan & Julien Smith - Trust Agents: Using the Web to Build Influence, Improve Reputation, and Earn Trust.
